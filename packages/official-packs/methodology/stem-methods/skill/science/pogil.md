# POGIL — Process-Oriented Guided Inquiry Learning

**POGIL** uses a structured activity sheet: learners work in teams (solo courseware:
learner-as-team) through **Explore → Concept Invention → Application**, building
content and process skills (communication, critical thinking). Meta-analytic
effect sizes are moderate-to-large vs traditional instruction (e.g. g ≈ 0.79 in
recent syntheses).

## When to use

- General chemistry, intro bio, engineering science courses with workbook rhythm
- When you want **explicit process goals** (not just content outcomes)
- Secondary lab units adapted to interactive sheets

## Unit arc

| Phase | Learner does | Block families |
|---|---|---|
| **Explore** | Guided questions on a phenomenon; collect patterns | **Manipulative** + **data**; short **narrative** prompts between steps |
| **Concept invention** | Name the pattern; connect to formal vocabulary | **Formalism**; **check** (discriminating MCQ) |
| **Application** | Transfer to unfamiliar setup | **Check** (numeric/mission), **executable** if quantitative |
| **Process wrap** | Reflect on strategy (optional in solo) | **Narrative** + light **check** |

## Rules

- Each "question" on the sheet should require an action (move a control, pick a
  graph, enter a number) — not only reading.
- Concept invention is *learner-derived* where possible; formalism confirms, not
  replaces, their wording.
- Application problems are **isomorphic in structure**, different in surface.

## Anti-patterns

- Explore phase that's actually a lecture with sliders.
- Application that's identical to Explore with new labels.
