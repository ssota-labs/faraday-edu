# Inquiry-based learning (IBL)

**Inquiry-based learning** (Lazonder & Harmsen 2016 meta-analysis; NSF-backed
math/science reforms) gives learners **ownership of questions and methods** with
scaffolding that fades. It spans disciplines when the goal is epistemic agency,
not a fixed algorithmic lesson shape.

## When to use

- Upper secondary seminars, undergrad discovery labs, honors tracks
- When standards allow **multiple valid paths** to an outcome
- *Not* a license for unstructured reading — guidance is light but intentional

## Unit arc

| Phase | Learner does | Block families |
|---|---|---|
| **Orient** | Encounter puzzling situation; form questions | **Narrative** + **manipulative** hook |
| **Question** | Select or refine investigable questions | **Check** (feasible vs not) |
| **Investigate** | Plan and carry out inquiry (open within bounds) | **Manipulative**, **data**, **executable** — learner choice among families |
| **Synthesize** | Patterns, claims, limitations | **Formalism** + **narrative** |
| **Share** | Present finding; respond to critique | **Presentation** or **check** peer-review style |

## Rules

- **Bounds** are real (time, tools, safety) — state them in orient phase.
- Scaffolding fades across units: early IBL gives tighter **check** gates;
  later units widen manipulative/executable choice.
- Synthesize must include **limitations** of the inquiry, not only triumph.

## Anti-patterns

- "Inquiry" = homework reading with no investigation.
- Zero scaffolding for true novices (expertise-reversal) — pair with worked
  examples from `lecture-design` when needed.

## Relation to other methods

| If… | Prefer… |
|---|---|
| Tight phenomenon, one cycle | POE |
| Model-building discipline | Modeling Instruction |
| Sheet-shaped team flow | POGIL |
| Open question ownership | IBL (this file) |
