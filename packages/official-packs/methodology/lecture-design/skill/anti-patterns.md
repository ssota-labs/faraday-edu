# Anti-patterns (commonly believed, poorly supported)

- **Passive review features** — highlighting, rereading, "summary at the end" as
  the main event: rated low-utility (Dunlosky et al. 2013). Fine as garnish, never
  the mechanism.
- **Learning-styles matching** (visual/auditory learners…): no adequate evidence
  (Pashler et al. 2008). Never build style-matched variants; match the
  *representation to the content* (spatial → 3D, process → stepped), not to a
  claimed style.
- **"2-sigma" claims** — Bloom's 1984 tutoring benchmark is contested (VanLehn
  2011: human tutoring d ≈ 0.79). Don't promise it in creator-facing copy.
- **Mechanical stacking** — techniques interact (e.g., self-explanation prompts
  can *blunt* worked-example gains — Barbieri 2023). Pick per lesson; don't bolt
  all five moves onto every node.
