# Learning design — outcomes without an LMS

Turn the intake brief into a tight teaching plan. **No roster, grades, or
cohort tools** — those are out of product scope.

## Decide

1. **Outcome** — what the learner can *do or see* after the lesson (one primary).
2. **Misconceptions** — the 1–3 traps this scene must surface and dissolve.
3. **Interaction thesis** — the manipulable variable + visible response that
   makes the concept obvious (detail in [interactive-design.md](interactive-design.md)).
4. **Check form** — how they prove the click (see [assessment.md](assessment.md)).

## Progression (single lesson)

For a single fullscreen lesson, keep progression inside the scene:

- **Entry** — predict or observe the naive state.
- **Manipulate** — change the primary variable; watch the model respond.
- **Formalize** — name the relationship (short overlay copy / label, not a slide deck).
- **Check** — a diagnostic ask that requires having used the controls.

Multi-lesson course maps and unlock graphs are optional later work — not v1
product surface. If you sequence several lessons, keep plans in the author’s
own notes; do not invent an LMS.

## Honest gates

A check the learner can guess-pass from the title is not a gate. Aim for
“passable after engaging the scene.”
